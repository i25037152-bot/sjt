Scenic Attractions

Where nature's grandeur meets ancient wonders

Jiuzhaigou Valley

Jiuzhaigou is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its spectacular multi-level waterfalls, vibrant turquoise lakes, and snow-capped peaks. Often described as a fairyland on earth, the valley's crystal-clear waters reflect a palette of blues, greens, and purples that change with the seasons.

Spanning over 72,000 hectares, this nature reserve is home to diverse wildlife including the giant panda and Sichuan golden snub-nosed monkey. Its ethereal beauty has made it one of China's most photographed and beloved natural wonders.

UNESCO World Heritage

Mount Emei

Mount Emei is one of the Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains of China, rising majestically to 3,099 meters above sea level. The mountain has been a place of spiritual significance for over 2,000 years, dotted with ancient temples, monasteries, and the awe-inspiring 71-meter-tall Golden Statue of Samantabhadra.

Visitors can witness the legendary "Emei Sea of Clouds" and the breathtaking "Buddha's Halo" — a natural optical phenomenon where a rainbow-colored ring appears around one's shadow in the mist.

Sacred Buddhist Mountain

Leshan Giant Buddha

Carved into a cliff face at the confluence of three rivers, the Leshan Giant Buddha stands 71 meters tall, making it the largest stone Buddha statue in the world. Constructed during the Tang Dynasty beginning in 713 AD, this monumental sculpture took over 90 years to complete.

The Buddha depicts Maitreya seated with his hands resting on his knees, gazing calmly over the rivers below. The sheer scale of the statue is humbling — a single toenail is large enough to seat a person.

Largest Stone Buddha

Dujiangyan Irrigation System

Built in 256 BC by the engineer Li Bing, Dujiangyan is the world's oldest and only surviving non-dam irrigation system. This ingenious engineering marvel still functions today, diverting water from the Minjiang River to irrigate the Chengdu Plain, transforming it into the fertile "Land of Abundance."

The system's design elegantly channels floodwaters while controlling sediment, demonstrating an extraordinary understanding of hydraulic engineering that was millennia ahead of its time.
